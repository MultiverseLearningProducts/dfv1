Intent

At Multiverse we aim to help you be professionally competent Data Analysts through teaching industry relevant tools and concepts you will use every day in your role. At the heart of our curriculum is the data analytics lifecycle, a north star principle which will help you to 'think data' throughout all aspects of your role and projects.



Learning is not a straight path, but a spiral where we continually build on and extend prior learning to help you gain new insights and fresh perspectives on how to conduct analysis. Throoughout the course we will return to techniques such as extracting, preparing, analysing, manipulating and visualising data through different tools and softwares such as Microsoft Excel, SQL, PowerBI, Python and R. At all stages we will review concepts and consider which tools would be best used to support your analysis, and justify these decisions in your portfolio.


In the latter stages of the course we will visit data science concepts such as machine learning that will allow you to build your own predictive models to leverage better insights. Throughout the course we will be increasing the complexity of the tools you will be using so you can apply your knowledge to increasingly complex situations.



Implementation

We employ a remote delivery model where lessons are taught over Zoom or Microsoft Teams, your experience is personalised through 1:1 sessions with your coach and the objectives you agree with them. Summative assessment is at the heart of our delivery and coaches will regularly check your understanding of the concepts taught through questions and software such as Kahoot! or Mentimeter.



Impact

The ultimate aim of this course is to prepare you for your End Point Assessment (EPA), upon succesful completion you will receive your Level 4 Data Analyst qualification accredited by the Institute for Apprenticeships and Technical Education. Through our delivery modules we will prepare you for the two exams on the course and teach you the skills you need to complete data projects in your role.


After the session on Excel you will be prepared to undertake projects at work that will have actual business impact in your organisation. These projects should be written up and evidenced in your portfolio. Incorporated throughout the course are sessions that will help you write your portfolio, by helping you identify evidence and write it in such a way that will demonstrate course standards while showing the business impact of your projects.


The End Point Assessment consists of a review of your portfolio, a final synoptic project and a one hour interview with an assessor. Your coach will help you prepare for this throughout the course.

DA: Excel

In the first technical module we will be looking at how Microsoft Excel can be used to wrangle and analyse data. We will explore tools such as VLOOKUP, INDEX-MATCH and pivot tables to leverage insights from data that will help you solve business problems in your organisation. At the end of this module is the first EPA prep session which will introduce the portfolio, the standards and how you document your projects.

Database Fundamentals

The second Multiverse delivery module focusses on database management. In your role you will access data from a variety of sources and this module will help you understand how they are designed, maintained and implemented. Key concepts such as normalisation and data modelling are explored while understanding the basic mechanices of common database management systems such as relational databases and NoSQL.

DA: SQL

Structured Query Language (SQL) is a tool used to retrieve information from relational databases. This module will explore key querying techniques, such as filtering, aggregation and joins. Advanced querying is introduced later as multiple joins and subqueries are utilised to gain better snapshots of our data. This module will give you the tools necessary to query databases in your organisation.

DA: PowerBI

Data visualisation is a core skill in any data analysts toolkit. They can be used to identify trends, display summary data and help understand the underlying patterns in our data. Softwares such as PowerBI and Tableau allow analysts to produce powerful and intuitive visualisations easily, without the need for coding. This module will teach you the how to use PowerBI to leverage insights from your data and present them in an attractive and logical manner. Note, you will either do PowerBI or Tableau which will have been discussed when you were onboarded.

DS: Modelling Basics

A key skill that should be demonstrated in your portfolio is linear regression, using basic algorithms to take advantage of underlying patterns to gain better insights from our data. This module will show you how to build basic regression and classification models and fine tune them by comparing performance metrics. Key concepts such as bias and variance are explored as you will learn how to develop models that will enable you to make predictions or evaluate the effect variables have on each other.
